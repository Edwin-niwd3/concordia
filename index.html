The Science of
Healing Through Music

Concordia is built on decades of peer-reviewed clinical music therapy research. Here is exactly how it helps specific conditions — and the science behind why.

🧠
Dementia & Alzheimer's
Music activates autobiographical memory even in advanced dementia — the hippocampus and limbic system remain responsive to familiar melodies long after verbal memory fades.
How Concordia helps: Memory Care mode uses familiar harmonic patterns (common cadences from the listener's era), 40 Hz gamma stimulation shown to activate memory-linked neural networks, slow tempos (60–68 BPM) to reduce agitation, and gentle timbres like piano or kalimba that feel warm and non-threatening. The MATCH clinical trial (Univ. Melbourne 2024) showed AI-personalised music significantly reduces agitation in dementia patients.
📄 MATCH Trial 2024 · LUCID ClinicalTrials.gov
💙
Depression
Music triggers dopamine release and lowers cortisol, directly addressing the neurochemical imbalances central to depression. A 2025 PMC meta-analysis found music therapy exerts a moderate-to-strong effect on reducing psychological stress.
How Concordia helps: Mood Lift mode uses major keys, brighter timbres, and a gentle ascending tempo (75–85 BPM) using the iso-principle — starting where you are emotionally and guiding you upward. Uplifting instruments like flute, harp, and marimba are selected automatically. Alpha binaural beats (8–14 Hz) are associated with reduced depressive symptoms.
📄 PMC Meta-Analysis 2025 · Psychology Today
😰
Anxiety & Panic
Anxiety is associated with chaotic brainwave activity. Therapeutic music slows the nervous system by entraining the brain toward calmer frequency states — neural oscillation synchronization.
How Concordia helps: Deep Calm mode uses theta binaural beats (4–8 Hz) and 60 BPM tempos to entrain and slow heart rate. Minor keys with descending melodic movement signal safety to the limbic system. Instruments with slow attacks (strings, singing bowl, crystal bowl) are especially effective for anxiety, as they avoid sharp transients that trigger startle responses.
📄 MDPI Binaural Review 2024 · By The Way Health 2025
😴
Insomnia & Sleep Disorders
Sleep onset is impaired by an overactive sympathetic nervous system. Music physically shifts the body into parasympathetic mode, lowering heart rate, blood pressure, and cortisol.
How Concordia helps: Sleep Prep mode uses delta binaural beats (0.5–4 Hz) and slow decelerating tempo. F major key, sparse melody, and instruments with long natural decay (singing bowl, crystal bowl, harp harmonics) create a sonic environment where the mind releases vigilance and drifts toward sleep naturally.
📄 By The Way Health 2025 · Cedars-Sinai Research
🌀
PTSD & Trauma
Trauma disrupts the brain's ability to distinguish safe from unsafe. Music can re-regulate the autonomic nervous system without requiring verbal re-processing of traumatic content.
How Concordia helps: Grounding mode avoids sudden dynamic changes or unexpected sounds. Predictable ostinato bass and steady rhythm give the nervous system a safe anchor. Instruments with warm, predictable timbres — piano, organ, kalimba — are prioritised. Theta waves support the calm, reflective state needed for emotional processing.
📄 UCSF Research 2025 · AI World Journal 2026
⚡
ADHD & Focus Difficulties
ADHD involves underactivation of the prefrontal cortex. Certain frequencies increase cortical arousal and improve sustained attention without medication.
How Concordia helps: Clarity mode uses alpha waves (8–14 Hz) and steady 72 BPM. Instruments with consistent timbres and minimal variation (organ, piano, marimba) reduce distraction. The AI avoids sudden dynamic changes that cause overstimulation. 40 Hz gamma is used in Memory Care specifically to improve working memory — a key deficit in ADHD too.
📄 MindBrain Institute 2025 · PMC 2025
🤝
Autism Spectrum (ASD)
Music bypasses verbal channels that can be challenging in ASD, offering a direct emotional connection pathway that feels safe and non-demanding.
How Concordia helps: Predictable musical structures with no sudden changes, low-vocal content, and consistent timbres. The instrument selector lets users choose exactly the tonal quality that feels comfortable — avoiding timbres that may cause sensory discomfort. Research shows musical training enhances communication and social interaction skills in children with ASD.
📄 PMC 2025 · Lim 2010 ASD Research
💊
Chronic Pain
Music activates the brain's endogenous opioid system — the same pathway as pain medication — reducing subjective pain perception without side effects.
How Concordia helps: Calming, slow music reduces cortisol and redirects attention from pain signals through attentional gating. Instruments with smooth, sustained tones (strings, flute, crystal bowl) are particularly effective as they don't create sharp transients that could increase body tension. Studies report pain reduction of 1–2 points on a 10-point scale.
📄 Cedars-Sinai 2025 · Integrative Medicine Review
